1. Boosting the On-State Current of Two-Dimensional Tunnel Field-Effect Transistors by Exploiting Interfacial Surface States
Abstract: A strategy is proposed to boost the on-state current (Ion) of two-dimensional tunnel field-effect transistors (2D TFETs) by exploiting interfacial surface states (ISSs) created at the electrode/channel interfaces. Taking WTe2-based 2D TFETs as a platform, first-principles and quantum-transport modeling approaches are performed to study the impact of 11 different ISSs on the device performance. It is demonstrated that the Ion of the ISS-TFETs can be boosted up to 2230 μA/μm, which is 4288% as large as that of the pristine TFETs. The underlying mechanism and the impact of ISS gap and channel length are also revealed.
Responsibility: This is my first research work. With the guide of Prof. Yuan Li, I propose a strategy to improve Ion of TFET. I calculate band structure, density of state and effective mass of WTe2 and IV curve of the TFET with ISSs by QuantumATK package. The paper has been submitted.
2. High Ion WTe2 TFET (come from surface state in channel/electrode interface) of 2229.85 μA/μm, which can meet the Ion goals of International Roadmap for Device and Systems (IRDS) for high performance (HP) devices. We introduce a surface state gap (SSG) bonded by van del waals bonds in channel/electrode interface. SSG can introduce surface state in on-state tunneling barrier to assist tunneling. Compared to normal trap-assist tunneling (TAT) that assist tunneling in a little energy. Surface state assist tunneling in almost all the bias window and get 1-2 orders of magnitude higher Ion than TAT TFET.
